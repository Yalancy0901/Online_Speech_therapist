Frequently Asked Questions

Speech therapy is specialized treatment to improve communication skills, covering speech, language, voice, fluency, and social communication.

Common reasons include speech sound disorders, language issues, stuttering, voice problems, cognitive-communication disorders, and social communication difficulties.

Sessions include assessment, goal setting, intervention (exercises and activities), and progress monitoring.

Techniques include articulation drills, language exercises, voice therapy, fluency shaping, cognitive-communication strategies, AAC methods, and social skills training.

Duration varies based on severity, progress, age, and motivation, ranging from weeks to ongoing support over years.

All ages benefit, addressing speech delays, language disorders, voice issues, or aphasia.
